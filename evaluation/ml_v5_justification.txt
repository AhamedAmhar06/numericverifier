GradientBoostingClassifier was selected over LogisticRegression for three reasons.
First, the signal interactions in P&L verification are non-linear: (coverage=1.0 AND
identity_fail=1) indicates REPAIR while (coverage=1.0 AND scale_mismatch=1) indicates
FLAG — the same coverage value has opposite implications depending on which constraint
fired. GBM learns these interactions through decision trees while LR treats them as
independent additive effects. Second, GBM provides built-in feature importance through
split frequency and gain, complemented by SHAP for per-prediction explanations —
producing auditable ML decisions aligned with the system's transparency goals. Third,
GBM is the standard approach in financial risk classification systems where tabular
structured features and interpretability are both required.

Signal design note: during V5 development, a leakage variable (claim_count) was
detected and removed. The synthetic FLAG answers were formatted as "Item in YEAR was
VALUE." producing claim_count=2 (year token + value token), while TAT-QA gold ACCEPT
answers are bare numerics (claim_count=1). This format artefact perfectly separated
classes by source rather than by error type, yielding spurious 100% accuracy.
Removing claim_count reduced to 10 features; the model retained strong performance
(CV 96.9% ± 3.6%) with no leakage.

SHAP findings (PermutationExplainer, test set):
- unsupported_claims_count (SHAP 0.176): strongest overall driver — ungrounded claims
  are the clearest FLAG signal
- pnl_identity_fail_count (SHAP 0.079 overall, dominant for REPAIR class): accounting
  identity violations are the primary REPAIR discriminator
- grounding_confidence_score (SHAP 0.045 overall, #2 for c2b FLAG cases at 0.163):
  composite grounding quality degrades with error size (0.863 at 2.1% error → 0.650
  at 3.8% error), providing the ML signal that enables V5 to catch all 16 c2b
  small_value_error cases that V4b missed
- near_tolerance_flag (SHAP 0.001 overall, #10 for c2b): fires correctly on all 16
  c2b cases as a binary indicator, but SHAP shows grounding_confidence_score
  carries more discriminative weight — the continuous confidence score is more
  informative than the binary flag alone

The near_tolerance_flag contributes as a binary gate (fires iff rel_err in
(tolerance, 0.10)) while grounding_confidence_score provides continuous
discrimination within the flagged region. Both signals together enabled V5 to
correctly classify 16/16 small_value_error ambiguous cases as FLAG (V4b: 0/16).

V5 on 84-case synthetic eval: 77/84 = 91.67%, matching rules-based system exactly.
The 7 remaining errors are structurally identical between V5 and rules — cases where
both rule triggers and signal patterns are ambiguous. V5's genuine advantage is on
the near-tolerance ambiguous zone (c2b cases) which the 84-case set does not include.
